The Programme

Unlike any other boarding in Switzerland

Rather than traditional dormitories, ISSH offers a neighbourhood-style, off-campus living experience in a safe, residential area just minutes from school. Each house accommodates a maximum of ten students, creating a homely environment where young people from around the world live and learn together.

  • Off-campus neighbourhood houses, not dormitories
  • Small groups of maximum 10 students per house
  • Co-ed houses with separate boys and girls floors
  • Fully equipped kitchens, lounges, and study areas
  • Centrally located, 10 minutes from campus

Student Life

Life beyond the classroom

A day in the life of an ISSH boarding student offers the perfect balance between structure and independence. The city itself becomes part of the learning experience.

Activities & Sports

Join local football teams, handball clubs, martial arts studios, or archery clubs. Schaffhausen offers the diversity of a large city with the safety of a small Swiss town.

Culture & Creativity

Take painting classes with Swiss artists, dive into regional culture through festivals and community events, or explore the historic Old Town and Rhine Falls.

Excursions & Trips

Visit CERN and the United Nations in Geneva, explore the Alps, enjoy water sports on the Rhine, or take weekend trips to Paris, Berlin, and Rome.

Weekend Life

Weekends balance rest and adventure. Students organise activities, explore Swiss culture, visit Zurich or Basel, and develop independence within clearly defined boundaries.

Outstanding academic results

ISSH boarders graduate not only with a globally recognised IB Diploma but also with the confidence, resilience, and life skills required to succeed at university and beyond. Students can also earn the US-accredited Middle States Association (MSA) High School Diploma, opening doors to universities worldwide.

Wellbeing & Safety

Where every student is seen and supported

ISSH takes a holistic approach to students' growth, considering academic, physical, social, and emotional wellbeing. Switzerland consistently ranks among the world's top ten countries for safety, and Schaffhausen's close-knit community of 40,000 residents creates an additional layer of natural security.

  • Qualified care professionals: healthcare, nutrition, pedagogy
  • Continuous supervision after school, overnight, and mornings
  • Houses in tranquil, family-oriented residential neighbourhoods
  • Proactive digital safety and media literacy guidance
  • Regular wellbeing discussions and stress-management support

A Day at ISSH Boarding

Structure meets independence

Daily routines provide the framework students need to thrive academically while fostering confidence and responsibility. No two students share the exact same schedule — everyone enjoys freedom to structure their free time.

Morning

Rise & prepare

Students wake, have breakfast, and prepare for the school day. The boarding team ensures everyone is ready and on time for classes.

School day

Academic programme

A full day of IB learning with small class sizes, dedicated teachers, and personalised attention at the ISSH campus.

Afternoon

Activities & study

Free time for sports, clubs, local activities, or supervised study. Students can explore Schaffhausen or relax at the boarding house.

Evening

Community time

Shared dinner, socialising, personal time, and preparation for the next day. Caregivers stay overnight for continuous support.

Independence & Growth

Skills for university and life

The ISSH boarding programme is designed around a structured yet flexible framework that allows students to naturally develop personal responsibility through real-life experience rather than strict regulations.

  • Cooking, budgeting, and household management
